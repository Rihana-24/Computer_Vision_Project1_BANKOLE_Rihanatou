Understanding Breast Cancer

Breast Cancer is a malignant tumor that starts in the cells of the breast. It can spread to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early.

Key Medical Concepts

  • Breast Cancer States: Stages 0 to IV describe the extent of cancer spread.
  • Glioma: A tumor from glial cells in the brain or spinal cord; may be aggressive (e.g., glioblastoma).
  • Meningioma: Usually benign tumors arising from the meninges; may cause headaches or neurological symptoms.
  • No Tumor: Indicates a healthy brain scan with no tumor present.
  • Pituitary Tumor: Tumor in the pituitary gland; can affect hormone balance and vision.

Prevention Tips for Breast Cancer

  • Get regular screenings (e.g., mammograms).
  • Exercise and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Limit alcohol and quit smoking.
  • Understand your family medical history.
  • Be alert to symptoms like lumps or changes in breast shape.

Tips to Reduce Risk of These Conditions

  • Avoid unnecessary radiation exposure (e.g., repeated CT scans).
  • Use protective gear when exposed to toxic chemicals.
  • Manage hormonal balance responsibly.
  • Adopt a brain-healthy lifestyle: sleep well, reduce stress, stay mentally active.
